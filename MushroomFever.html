Mushroom Fever by Phyllis Ma explores the history of the first New York Mycological Club. Established in 1897 by Cornelia C. Bedford, a cooking teacher and food writer, the club was a haven for mycophiles to study and eat wild fungi. Many social events were hosted around New York City—including bicycle forays, cooking demonstrations and Chinatown mushroom dinners. After a few exhilarating years, the club dissolved and its demise remains a mystery.
